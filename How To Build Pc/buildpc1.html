How To Build Your PC

Pick a compatible CPU, motherboard, RAM, GPU, storage, power supply, and case. Check compatibility and performance.

Clean, static-free area with tools ready: screwdriver, anti-static wristband, thermal paste, and more.

Open the motherboard socket carefully, insert the CPU, secure it, and install your RAM sticks properly.

Place the motherboard inside the case and screw it onto standoffs to avoid short circuits.

Secure the PSU and route the cables for power delivery to components.

Mount SSDs/HDDs in designated bays and install your graphics card into the PCIe slot.

Connect power, data cables, case buttons, USB, and fans carefully according to your motherboard manual.

Boot your PC, enter BIOS to verify settings, and install your operating system.
